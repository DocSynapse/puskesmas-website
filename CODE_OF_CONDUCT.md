## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of this project, pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are building software that directly impacts patient care and clinical workflows. This responsibility demands that we hold ourselves to the highest standards of professional conduct, both in our code and in our interactions with one another.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the project and its end users (healthcare professionals and patients).
- Show empathy toward other community members.
- Prioritize patient safety and clinical accuracy in all technical decisions.
- Handle patient data and clinical information with the utmost care and confidentiality.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional healthcare technology setting.
- Deliberately introducing code that could compromise patient safety or data integrity.
- Sharing or mishandling clinical data, credentials, or protected health information.

---

## Healthcare-Specific Standards

Given the clinical nature of this project, contributors must additionally:

1. **Respect Patient Confidentiality** -- Never include real patient data in code, commits, issues, or documentation. Use anonymized or synthetic data for testing and development.

2. **Prioritize Clinical Safety** -- When in doubt about whether a code change could affect clinical decision support, diagnostic accuracy, or patient safety, raise the concern before proceeding.

3. **Acknowledge Scope of Practice** -- This software supports clinical workflows but does not replace medical judgment. Code and documentation should consistently reinforce this boundary.

4. **Comply with Regulations** -- All contributions must be consistent with applicable healthcare regulations, including Indonesian health data protection standards.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at Sentra Healthcare Solutions. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances.

Project maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

### Enforcement Actions

1. **Correction** -- A private written warning with clarity on the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.
2. **Warning** -- A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary Ban** -- A temporary ban from any interaction or contribution to the project.
4. **Permanent Ban** -- A permanent ban from any interaction or contribution to the project.
